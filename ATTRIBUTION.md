# Attribution

## Methods

CutoffGrade Studio implements Ken Lane's economic definition of ore. The methods are attributable to:

- **Lane, K. F. (1964).** Choosing the optimum cut-off grade. *Colorado School of Mines Quarterly*, 59(4), 811–829. —
  the limiting cut-offs + the opportunity-cost insight.
- **Lane, K. F. (1988).** *The Economic Definition of Ore: Cut-off Grades in Theory and Practice.* Mining Journal
  Books, London. — the full theory + the iterative NPV optimization.
- **Dagdelen, K. (1992).** Cutoff grade optimization. *Proc. 23rd APCOM Symposium*, SME, 157–165. — the median
  construction of the effective optimum.
- **Asad, M. W. A. & Topal, E. (2011).** Net present value maximization model for optimum cut-off grade policy of open
  pit mining operations. *Journal of the SAIMM*, 111(11), 741–750.
- **Hall, B. (2014).** *Cut-off Grades and Optimising the Strategic Mine Plan.* AusIMM, Melbourne.
- **Rendu, J.-M. (2014).** *An Introduction to Cut-off Grade Estimation,* 2nd ed. SME, Englewood, Colorado.

Full bibliography in `frontend/src/data/citations.ts` and the in-app Methodology page.

## Data

All deposits + economics are **synthetic** — a porphyry-copper-like base case with a lognormal grade distribution,
generated by `frontend/src/lane/cases.ts` + the `gen_train.mjs` sampler. No real deposit or economic data is used or
redistributed. The `C-UNIFORM` and `C-BREAKEVEN` cases are closed-form analytic controls.
